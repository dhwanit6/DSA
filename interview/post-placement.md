# Post-Placement — Your First 90 Days & Beyond

> Getting the offer is not the finish line. It's the starting line. The gap between "campus hire" and "productive engineer" is where careers are made or stalled.

---

## Understanding Your Offer Letter

| Component | What It Is | Indian Campus Context |
|---|---|---|
| **Base Salary (Fixed CTC)** | Guaranteed monthly pay | This is the number that matters most for freshers |
| **Variable / Bonus** | Performance-linked pay (10-20% of CTC) | Not guaranteed. Don't count on it for year 1 |
| **ESOPs / RSUs** | Stock options (startups) or restricted stock (FAANG) | Startup ESOPs: assume 0 value until IPO. FAANG RSUs: real money, vesting over 4 years |
| **Joining Bonus** | One-time payment at joining | Often clawed back if you leave within 1 year |
| **Benefits** | Insurance, food, relocation | Read the fine print — health insurance coverage varies wildly |

**CTC vs In-Hand:** In India, CTC includes employer PF contribution, gratuity, insurance premiums. Your actual monthly in-hand is typically 65-75% of monthly CTC. Always ask for the salary breakup.

## Comparing Offers

| Factor | MNC (Amazon, Google, Microsoft) | Startup (Series B+) | Service Company (TCS, Infosys) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Base salary | High (15-40 LPA for top campuses) | Medium-High (15-30 LPA) + ESOPs | Lower (3.5-8 LPA) |
| Learning curve | Deep in specific domain | Broad across many areas | Slow, process-heavy |
| Career growth | Structured levels (SDE1 → SDE2 → SDE3) | Fast but unpredictable | Slow, seniority-based |
| Work-life balance | Team-dependent | Generally demanding | Generally relaxed |
| Resume value | Very high for 2-3 years | High if company succeeds | Moderate |
| **Best for** | Structured growth, deep technical skills | Breadth, ownership, speed | Stability, visa sponsorship |

## First 90 Days — What Actually Matters

### Week 1-2: Listen and Learn
- Set up your dev environment completely. Don't ask for help on something you can Google
- Read all onboarding docs, wikis, and past design docs for your team
- Understand the codebase: what does each service/module do? What's the deployment pipeline?
- Identify 2-3 people who seem helpful and approachable

### Week 3-4: First Contribution
- Pick a small bug or task from the backlog. Fix it. Get it code-reviewed and merged
- The goal is NOT to impress — it's to learn the code review process and deployment flow
- Ask questions about WHY things are done a certain way, not just HOW

### Month 2: Build Trust
- Take on a medium-sized feature. Own it end-to-end (design → implement → test → deploy)
- Start writing documentation for things you found confusing — your fresh perspective is valuable
- Participate in code reviews — even commenting "I learned something new from this approach" builds visibility

### Month 3: Establish Your Identity
- You should now be able to independently pick up and complete tasks
- Start having opinions in design discussions (backed by data, not just preferences)
- Identify one area you want to go deep in — databases, performance, testing, ML — and start building expertise

## Keep Your DSA Sharp (Maintenance Mode)

| Activity | Frequency | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Solve 2 LeetCode problems | Per week | 1 hour |
| Participate in 1 contest (CF/LC) | Per month | 2 hours |
| Review patterns journal | Monthly | 30 min |

**Why this matters:** When you want to switch companies in 2-3 years, you'll need to interview again. Starting from scratch takes 3-6 months. Maintaining takes 1 hour/week.
