# Final Review — The 3-Day Pre-Interview Checklist

> You have an interview on Thursday. It's Monday evening. Here's exactly what to do.

---

## Day 1 (3 days before): Pattern Refresh

- [ ] Re-read [Cheatsheets](../topics/cheatsheets.md) — all 11 topic sections. Circle anything you've forgotten
- [ ] Re-solve 3 problems you previously solved but struggled with (from your patterns journal)
- [ ] Review the [DP Pattern Taxonomy](../topics/cheatsheets.md) — can you name all 10 patterns?
- [ ] Run through your [Code Templates](../topics/code-templates.md) — can you write DSU, Dijkstra, and Binary Search from memory?

## Day 2 (2 days before): Interview Simulation

- [ ] Do 1 full mock session from [Mock Sessions](../interview/mock-sessions.md) — timed 45 min, talk aloud
- [ ] Score yourself on the rubric. If any dimension < 3, focus your remaining time there
- [ ] Review [Interview Mechanics](../interview/mechanics.md) — the 45-minute framework and stuck recovery protocol
- [ ] Practice your 90-second "Tell Me About Yourself" in front of a mirror. Time it
- [ ] Run through your 8 behavioral stories — can you tell each in < 2 minutes?

## Day 3 (1 day before): Company-Specific & Logistics

- [ ] Read the [Company-Specific](../interview/company-specific.md) section for your target company
- [ ] Read the **last 10 interview experiences** for this company on LeetCode Discuss and GFG
- [ ] Note the most common topics and patterns from those reports
- [ ] Prepare 3 questions to ask the interviewer (see [questions list](../interview/behavioral.md))
- [ ] Check your equipment: webcam, mic, stable internet, IDE or CoderPad logged in
- [ ] Set 2 alarms for interview morning

## Interview Morning

- [ ] Wake up 2 hours before the interview
- [ ] Light review of [Cheatsheets](../topics/cheatsheets.md) — 20 min max. Don't learn anything new
- [ ] Solve 1 easy problem as warm-up. Something you've done before. Gets your brain into code mode
- [ ] Review your "Tell Me About Yourself" one last time
- [ ] 15 min before: close all tabs, open only the interview platform, have water and pen/paper ready

---

## The 7 Things to Remember During the Interview

1. **Clarify BEFORE solving.** Ask about constraints, edge cases, expected output format
2. **State brute force first.** Even if you know the optimal — it shows systematic thinking
3. **Announce your approach** before writing code. "I'll use a hash map for O(1) lookups"
4. **Name variables meaningfully.** `left`, `right`, `maxSum` — not `i`, `j`, `x`
5. **Test your code** after writing. Trace through given example + 1 edge case aloud
6. **If stuck:** say "Let me reconsider the approach" — not silence. Narrate your thought process
7. **Time awareness:** If 20 min in and no code written, start implementing your best approach now

---

## After the Interview

- [ ] Write down every question you were asked (while it's fresh — do this within 30 min)
- [ ] Note what went well and what didn't
- [ ] If you froze on a problem, solve it properly NOW — you'll see similar problems in future rounds
- [ ] Don't post-mortem obsessively. What's done is done. Move to prepping the next round
