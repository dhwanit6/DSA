## 2) Choose Your Track (30 Seconds)

| Situation | Track |
|---|---|
| 4th/5th sem, internship cycle, 4-8 months | Internship |
| Full-time prep with 8+ months | Full-Time |

## 3) Internship Track (6-8 Months)

### Exact Order

1. Phase 0
2. Phase 1
3. Phase 2
4. Interview Mechanics
5. OA Strategy
6. CS Fundamentals Roadmap
7. DBMS & SQL
8. Operating Systems
9. Computer Networks
10. Concurrency & Multithreading
11. Mock Sessions
12. Company-Specific Prep
13. Behavioral
14. Final Review

### Monthly Map

1. Month 1: Phase 0 + first 20 problems.
2. Months 2-3: Phase 1.
3. Month 4: Phase 2 core.
4. Month 5: Mechanics + OA + DBMS.
5. Month 6: OS + Networks.
6. Month 7: Concurrency + mocks + company-specific.
7. Month 8: Behavioral + final review.

### Why This Works

1. DSA remains the primary filter.
2. DBMS, OS, CN, and concurrency are added only after your coding base is moving.
3. This matches how many Indian product interviews, European backend interviews, and mixed-market placements actually feel in practice.

2. Months 5-8: Phase 2.
3. Months 9-10: Phase 3.
4. Month 11: DBMS + OS.
5. Month 12: Networks + Concurrency.
6. Month 13: System design.
7. Month 14: LLD + estimation.
8. Months 15-16: Mocks + company-specific.
9. Months 17-18: Final revision and interviews.

### Why This Works

1. Full-time prep rewards deeper DSA coverage.
2. Design rounds become much easier after DBMS, OS, networks, and concurrency are stable.
3. This matches the stronger backend and product-company interview pattern better than pure LeetCode grinding.

---

## 5) Crash Track (Under 4 Months)

Use [Crash Plans](../phases/crash-plans.md), but do not ignore CS fundamentals.

Minimum crash sequence:

1. Crash Plans
2. Mechanics
3. OA Strategy
4. CS Fundamentals Roadmap
5. DBMS + OS + Networks quick revision
6. Mock Sessions
7. Final Review

If you skip fundamentals entirely, direct interview rounds become unstable.
