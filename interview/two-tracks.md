# 🗺 The Two Tracks — Internship Prep vs Full-Time Placement

> This chapter is not in most guides. Internship and full-time interviews are different animals.

---

## Track A — Internship (Target: Month 6-8)

Internship interviews are shorter, more focused, and genuinely easier than full-time rounds.

| Dimension | Internship | Full-Time |
|---|---|---|
| **DSA Difficulty** | Easy to Medium (rarely Hard) | Medium to Hard |
| **Rounds** | 1 OA + 1-2 Technical | 1 OA + 3-5 Technical |
| **System Design** | Rare, conceptual | Expected at good companies |
| **Behavioral** | Light — "tell me about a project" | Deep — STAR with quantified impact |
| **Code Quality** | Basic correctness + communication | Full code quality + edge cases + testing |

### Track A Milestones
- **Month 1-2:** Phase 0 (C++ STL) + Arrays, Strings, Binary Search, Recursion
- **Month 3:** Linked Lists, Trees, Basic Graphs. Start Pramp mock interviews
- **Month 4-5:** Basic DP (1D, 2D), Greedy. LeetCode company-tagged mediums
- **Month 6+:** OA practice. 2 timed mock OAs per week

---

## Track B — Full-Time Placement (Target: Month 14-18)

Full-time is a different level. Companies now expect system design basics even from freshers.

### Requirements:
- Phase 1-3 of DSA curriculum fully completed
- System design basics understood and practiced
- Estimation problems fluent — latency numbers memorized
- Behavioral stories with quantified impact fully prepared
- Company-specific preparation done in final 4-6 weeks

---

## 📊 The Internship Advantage

> India Skills Report 2026: Companies using internship-to-hire pipelines report **44% higher 5-year retention.**

Internships are no longer just experience — they ARE the interview for a full-time role. If you are in 4th semester, your internship season begins in 5th semester (August-October). That gives you 6-8 months.
