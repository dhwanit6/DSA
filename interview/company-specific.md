# Company-Specific Prep

> Company prep is pattern frequency plus interview style, not logo worship.

---

## 1) Start With Market, Not Brand

These are broad tendencies, not hard rules:

| Market | Common emphasis | What to train harder |
|---|---|---|
| US big tech and strong product companies | coding quality, communication, testing, behavioral, role-based design | clean interview flow, medium/hard DSA, tradeoff language |
| European product, fintech, and backend teams | practical coding, APIs, SQL, debugging, concurrency, service reasoning | applied CS fundamentals, backend tradeoffs, concise design |
| Indian product companies and placements | OA speed, DSA, DBMS, OS, CN, OOP, LLD, direct viva questions | fast theory answers, SQL writing, pattern recall, company-tagged sets |

Do not memorize one market and assume all companies behave that way.

---

## 2) Build Your Target List

1. Choose your top 5-10 companies.
2. Group them by interview style:
   - coding heavy
   - backend fundamentals heavy
   - design heavy
   - behavior heavy
3. For each company, collect:
   - 15-20 tagged problems
   - likely theory areas
   - one-page notes on round structure

---

## 3) Weekly Prep Loop

1. Solve 5 company-tagged problems.
2. Review 1 theory cluster that fits that company:
   - DBMS
   - OS
   - Networks
   - Concurrency
   - System design
3. Practice one company-style behavioral story.
4. Write a short debrief:
   - repeated patterns
   - repeated mistakes
   - timing issues

---

## 4) Adjust By Role

### General SWE / New Grad

Prioritize:

1. DSA medium problems
2. interview mechanics
3. behavioral
4. fundamentals that can be answered in 45-60 seconds

### Backend / Platform

Prioritize harder:

1. DBMS
2. OS
3. Networks
4. concurrency
5. system design

### Startup / Lean Teams

Prioritize:

1. implementation clarity
2. practical debugging
3. API/service tradeoffs
4. ownership stories

---

## 5) The Mistake To Avoid

Do not prepare "Google prep," "Amazon prep," and "European prep" as three unrelated systems.

Instead:

1. keep one DSA base
2. keep one CS fundamentals base
3. adjust the last 20 percent for company style

That is how strong candidates stay consistent across markets.
