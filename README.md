## 🔍 What Makes This Different

Every DSA guide on GitHub shares the same problem: **they're resource lists, not learning systems.** They tell you *what* to study. They never tell you *how* to think while studying, *how* to perform under pressure, or *how* to recover when you fall behind.

This guide was built after studying:
- [coding-interview-university](https://github.com/jwasham/coding-interview-university) (300k+ stars) — best CS checklist, but outdated and US-focused
- [r/developersIndia Wiki](https://wiki.developersindia.in/faqs/how-to-start-dsa) — most trusted Indian student resource
- [Neetcode Roadmap](https://neetcode.io/roadmap) — best pattern-based approach
- [Tech Interview Handbook](https://www.techinterviewhandbook.org/) — best interview mechanics resource
- 50+ real interview experience posts from LeetCode Discuss, r/cscareerquestions, and GFG (2024-26)

**What this guide adds that none of the above have:**
- Explicit **interview mechanics** (the 45-minute framework, what to say when stuck)
- **OA strategy** as its own chapter — the first gate most guides ignore
- **Internship vs full-time** distinction with different preparation paths  
- **Company-specific** differentiation — Google ≠ Amazon ≠ TCS
- **Pattern recognition** — how to identify *which* algorithm to use, not just how algorithms work
- **Complexity analysis as a skill** — derivation, not just labeling
- **A real patterns journal template** — what a good entry looks like
- **Mental game & recovery** — because every 18-month journey hits hard months

## 🎯 The Core Philosophy

> *You are not preparing to solve LeetCode problems. You are building three types of intelligence.*

**Pattern Intelligence** — See a new problem and map it to a known approach in < 30 seconds.  
Builds through: topic-clustered practice + patterns journal + spaced review.

**Systems Intelligence** — Understand how code behaves at scale. Reason about 5TB files and billion-user systems.  
Builds through: estimation problems + system design study + latency numbers memorized cold.

**Communication Intelligence** — Explain your thinking clearly under pressure. Make your reasoning visible.  
Builds through: mock interviews from Month 3, talking out loud while coding alone, the 45-minute interview framework.

> The FAANG interview evaluates all four dimensions simultaneously: Communication, Problem Solving, Technical Competency, and Testing. Most candidates only practice one. That is why most candidates fail.

## ❗ Critical Rules (Read Before Starting)

**Rule 1:** Never look at a solution before 30-45 minutes of genuine struggle. The struggle is the neurological mechanism of learning, not an obstacle to it.

**Rule 2:** After every problem you solve, write a 3-sentence patterns journal entry. No entry = you don't own the insight yet. See [Journal Template](./topics/patterns-journal-template.md).

**Rule 3:** Start talking out loud while coding from Week 1. Alone, to yourself, to a wall. The habit of externalizing reasoning takes months to build and can't be rushed.

**Rule 4:** Participate in live contests from Month 3 onward. Timed pressure is a different neurological state from peaceful practice. You must train it separately.

**Rule 5:** Read the editorial for every contest problem — including the ones you solved correctly. There is almost always a cleaner approach. This is where top 1% separate from top 10%.
