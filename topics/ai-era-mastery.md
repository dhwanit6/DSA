# AI-Era Interview Mastery

> AI tools changed the workflow, not the scoring rubric. You are still evaluated on reasoning and communication.

---

## 1) New Interview Reality

1. Some rounds allow AI assistance or code completion.
2. Most rounds still require live reasoning and ownership.
3. Blind copy from AI is easier to detect than most candidates think.

---

## 2) Safe AI Usage Protocol

1. Attempt first for 20-30 minutes.
2. Ask AI for one hint, not full solution.
3. Re-derive solution in your own words.
4. Re-code from scratch without looking.
5. Explain complexity and edge cases aloud.

---

## 3) Worked Example (Using AI Correctly)

Problem: LC 560 (Subarray Sum Equals K)

1. You attempt and get stuck on prefix-map update order.
2. Ask AI: "What invariant should hold at each index?"
3. AI explains invariant concept only.
4. You implement independently and test on edge cases.
5. You journal trigger: prefix sum + frequency map.

Outcome: you learned the invariant, not just the code.

---

## 4) Common Failure Modes and Fixes

| Failure mode | Risk | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Asking AI for full code immediately | Zero retention | Ask for hint or pseudocode only |
| Not validating AI output | Hidden wrong complexity | Always dry run and analyze complexity |
| Memorizing AI phrasing | Weak interview communication | Explain in your own language |
| Overusing AI on easy tasks | Dependency | Keep 2-3 no-AI sessions weekly |

---

## 5) Weekly AI Discipline Checklist

- [ ] 2 sessions with zero AI usage.
- [ ] 2 sessions with hint-only AI usage.
- [ ] 1 post-session note on what AI helped with and what it did not.
